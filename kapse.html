The Syrian cuisine is famous for the Kabsa dish, which is always prepared with long-grained basmati rice,
and is often the main meal at invitations, banquets and occasions.

  • Heat the ghee in a saucepan over medium heat Add onions, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom and lime. My heart mix until onions wither.
  • Add the chicken and green pepper, and cook, stirring occasionally, until tender.
  • Add the tomatoes, then let the mixture cook for 20 minutes. Add the tomato sauce, bay leaf and Kabsa seasoning.
  • Add coriander, black pepper and cumin. My heart mix again.
  • Add an amount of water, and leave the pot to cook on the stove, until the chicken is completely cooked. Lift the chicken out of the broth and transfer it to an oven tray.
  • Red the chicken in the preheated oven, at a medium temperature.
  • Add the rice to the broth, let it cook and stir, seasoning with salt if desired.
  • Reduce the heat, then cover the pot so that the rice absorbs all the liquid, then transfer it to a serving plate and spread the chicken pieces over it.
